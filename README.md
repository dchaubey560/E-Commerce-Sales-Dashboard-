# Diwali Sales Analysis

## About the Project

Diwali is one of the biggest shopping festivals in India, making it an ideal opportunity to analyze customer purchasing behavior. In this project, I explored a Diwali sales dataset to understand who the customers are, what they buy, and which factors contribute the most to overall sales.

The project focuses on cleaning the data, performing exploratory data analysis (EDA), and creating meaningful visualizations that help uncover business insights.

---

## Objectives

The main goals of this project were to:

- Clean and prepare the dataset for analysis.
- Explore customer demographics.
- Identify purchasing trends.
- Understand which states, occupations, and product categories generate the highest sales.
- Present the findings using clear and interactive visualizations.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains customer information collected during Diwali sales, including:

- Customer ID
- Gender
- Age
- Age Group
- Marital Status
- State
- Occupation
- Product Category
- Number of Orders
- Purchase Amount

## Key Findings

Some interesting insights from the analysis include:

- Female customers contributed more to overall sales than male customers.
- Customers aged **26–35 years** were the most active buyers.
- Married women showed higher purchasing power during the festival season.
- Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Karnataka recorded the highest sales.
- Customers working in the IT, Healthcare, and Aviation sectors spent more on average.
- Food, Clothing, and Electronics were among the most popular product categories.
